    [經濟學人選讀] Still in danger - the world economy

    Benson Chiu
    2023-07-24
    English
    GRE, the economist, world economy
    • 原文網址：The world economy is still in danger (economist.com)
    • 閱讀難度：⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

    Vocabulary

    palpable

    • 可感知的，易察覺的；觸摸得到的

    all the more

    • Even more than before
    • 更加、愈發、格外

    greenback

    • 美元

    commodity

    • 商品、貨物

    materialize

    • 實現、成為現實

    liquefy / liquify

    • 使成為液體、使液化

    one-off

    • something that happens or is made or done only once
    • 一次性的事物、一次性的
    • one-off decline in energy prices

    grab headlines

    • 成為重要的頭條新聞

    tacit

    • understood without being expressed directly
    • 默識的、不明言的

    shed

    • （常爲報刊用語）去除，擺脫

    stall

    • 停滯

    ominously

    • 惡兆地、不祥地、不吉利地

    hoard

    • 貯藏、囤積

    should

    • 萬一…的話，如果…的話
    • Should companies decide that it is too costly to cling to workers who may or may not be needed in the future, then layoﬀs could rise abruptly.

    cling to sth

    • 堅持，保持，死死抱住（信念或希望）

    barely

    • 僅、剛好、幾乎沒有

    contend with sth

    • to have to deal with a difficult or unpleasant situation
    • 必須處理，必須應對（困境或不愉快的事）

    weighed down

    • 使（某人）焦慮；使（某人）沮喪

    By all means

    • （表示許可）好的，當然可以

    unscathed

    • 未受傷的，無恙的

    divergence

    • the situation in which two things become different
    • 差異；分歧

    weigh sb down

    • to make a person feel worried and unhappy because of problems, responsibilities, and duties
    • 使（某人）焦慮；使（某人）沮喪

    Summary

    Parapraph 1

    通貨膨脹正在趨緩，美國聯準會連帶各國的央行往後會採取更寬鬆的貨幣政策

    • …… but today their good cheer is palpable.
    • Yet news that America’s headline rate of annual inﬂation fell to 3% in June has fed hopes that the Fed’s next rate rise, （which is expected on July 26th）, will be its last and that other central banks might relax, too.

    Paragraph 2

    世界的經濟成長同時也在趨緩 —— 中國儘管被預測在放棄清零政策後會有蓬勃的成長，卻僅在第二季度相較於第一季成長了 0.8%；此外，全球製造業也因為疫情的終結，消費者對家用、辦公用品的需求降低而趨於蕭條；最後，美國就算在上半年擁有強勁的經濟成長，大部分的預測還是認為經濟成長會逐漸趨緩。

    • The surge of hope is all the more unusual because the world economy is slowing down. [主旨句]

    Paragraph 3

    預測家預測經濟會趨緩，但不會萎縮（負成長），這對美國的通膨恢復是最有利的。中國緩步的經濟成長也意味著全球商品的價格上漲尚未顯現，間接地幫助了正在進行能源轉型的歐洲。

    • Increasingly, however, they are not expecting it to shrink. -> growth cooling…
    • Even the disappointing reopening in China (, which does not have an inﬂation problem of its own, ) has meant a feared surge in global commodities prices has not materialised.

    Paragraph 4 ~ 7

    然而，就上面的原因就認定全球經濟會「軟著陸」是一個錯誤的想法。筆者舉出三項原因：

    • 物價指數年增率仍然高於各國央行所設下的 2% 目標。對於富裕國家，距離通膨的結束還有一大段路要走，且經濟學家認為這一段路是最艱難的。

    The ﬁrst is that inﬂation, though lower, remains far above central banks’ 2% targets. …… In other words, the rich world has some way to go before it is fully disinﬂated and many economists expect the last mile to be the hardest.

    • 儘管經濟趨緩 (cooling off) 的好處在短期可見，在長期可能會付出代價。
      • 勞動市場：
        • 職缺減緩有停滯的現象
        • 各大企業有囤積員工的現象 -> 經濟趨緩、成本提高下潛在的解僱風險

    The second risk is that, whereas the world is seeing the beneﬁts of cooling oﬀ now, the costs may not be visible for a while.

    Yet nobody knows for how long the jobs market can shed fat rather than muscle—and in recent months the fall in job openings has stalled ominously.

    Should (萬一…的話，如果…的話) companies decide that it is too costly to cling to workers who may or may not be needed in the future, then layoﬀs could rise abruptly.

    • 世界各大經濟體之間的狀況分歧。
      • 英國：工資與價格上漲 -> a troubling outlier
      • 日本：幾乎沒有緊縮的貨幣政策
      • 中國：因為壞債 (Bad debt) 所造成的焦慮，其將會面對「結構性的經濟趨緩」

    The third danger is that divergence among the world’s big economies means that even as the pressure on the Fed lifts, policymakers else where remain worried.

    Paragraph 8

    總結：通膨的走向仍然充滿巨大的不確定性，在慶祝現在好消息的同時，也須注意世界的經濟情勢未必會完全安好無恙。

    Wherever you look, in other words, there remains immense uncertainty about where inﬂation and interest rates will eventually settle. By all means celebrate good news. But the world economy has not yet escaped unscathed.
