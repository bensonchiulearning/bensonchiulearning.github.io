    [經濟學人選讀] Artificial intelligence - Machine dreams

    Benson Chiu
    2023-07-26 (Updated: 2023-07-27)
    English
    AI, GRE, The Economist
    • 原文網址：Your employer is (probably) unprepared for artificial intelligence (economist.com)
    • 閱讀難度：⭐⭐⭐

    Vocabularies

    tractor

    • 拖拉機

    fever pitch

    • a state of very strong emotion
    • 極度興奮；極為激動；狂熱

    diffusion

    • the action of spreading in many directions
    • 擴散;傳播;散佈

    bar

    • 除了 … 之外
    • Japan is unusually innovative, producing on a per­person basis more patents a year than any country bar South Korea.

    periphery

    • the outer edge of an area
    • 周邊，邊緣，外圍

    bureaucracy

    • a system for controlling or managing a country, company, or organization that is operated by a large number of officials employed to follow rules carefully
    • 官僚作風；官僚主義；官僚體制

    two-tier

    • used to describe something that is organized in two levels or stages
    • 兩級的；雙層的；雙重的

    frontier firms

    • OECD defines frontier firms as those in the top 10% of the productivity distribution

    pull away

    • 迅速躲開，閃避（某人）

    chunk

    • a roughly cut piece
    • 大塊;厚塊

    Business dynamism

    • relates to measures of birth, growth and decline of businesses and its impact on employment.
    • is a vital process to continued productivity and sustained economic growth.

    utilities

    • 公用事業：電力、供水、廢物處理、污水處理、燃氣供應、交通、通訊等

    break the mould/mold

    • to be new and different
    • 打破常規

    Summary

    Paragraph 1

    以拖拉機的發明與發展舉例，說明其從發明到普及花費了非常多時間

    • All agree, though, that the tractor took a long time to make a mark.

    Paragraph 2

    大家對人工智慧發展前景的推測極為樂觀。然而，這項技術目前仍無法發揮其經濟影響力，因為大部分公司皆未採用這項技術去做全面的改革。

    • And yet ai’s economic impact will be muted unless millions of ﬁrms beyond tech centres like Silicon Valley adopt it.

    Paragraph 3

    兩國的案例：日本和法國

    • 日本：有非比尋常的創新能力，然而在技術的傳播上表現不佳。
      • According to our analysis, Japan is roughly 40% poorer than would be expected based on its innovation
    • 法國：創新能力普通，但善於散播知識

    Paragraph 4 ~ 6

    用上述兩國作為範例，描述世界經濟發展的概況：

    • 19 和 20 世紀：全球各企業更像法國，新興科技散播速度快

    • 21 世紀：漸趨如第三段日本般的情形

    • During the 19th and 20th centuries businesses around the world became more French, with new technologies diﬀusing ever faster.

    • Since the mid 2000s, however, the world has been turning Japanese.

    舉例：美國企業使用新技術的情形 (PG-5)、日本地方銀行 (PG-6)、英國進口的 ﬂoppy disks, MiniDiscs and cassettes (PG-6)、堅持紙本的政府機關

    Paragraph 7

    當今的結果呈現兩極化：

    • 英國：生產力在前 10% 的企業產值從 98,000 英鎊上升至 108,500 英鎊，後 10% 在停滯狀態
    • 加拿大：1990 年代，生產力在前 10% 的企業 (frontier firms) 比後 10% 生產力成長高了 40%，2000 至 2015 這個差距增加到了三倍之多
    • 美國：2017 年生產力前 25% 企業的 ROIC (資本回報率) 比中位數高了 20%，差距是 2000 年的兩倍
    • The result is a two-tier economy. Firms that embrace tech are pulling away from the competition.
    • Some companies see huge gains from buying new tech; many see none at all.

    Paragraph 8

    科技擴散的過慢對現實生活帶來的後果，使用舊科技的人們可能會遭受薪水下降的後果，進而加深貧富差距。

    • Although the economics can sound abstract, the realworld consequences are familiar.
    • “the majority of inequality growth across workers is due to increasing average wage diﬀerences between ﬁrms”.

    Paragraph 9

    解釋科技擴散慢速的三個原因

    1. 新興科技的性質
    2. 停滯的競爭
    3. 逐漸趨嚴的法令規範
    • As technological progress becomes more incremental, diffusion also slows, since companies have less incentive and face less competitive pressure to upgrade.
    • Electricity v.s. Cloud Computing and Machine Learning

    Paragraph 10

    21 世紀初，富裕國家的商業動能降低 —— 人口老化、新公司數量銳減、員工流動率低等因素導致了新興科技緩慢的擴散速度

    • Business dynamism fell…… All this reduced diﬀusion, since workers spread tech and business practices as they move across the economy.

    Paragraph 11

    政府經營或介入較高的產業具有較低的科技變革速度。政客有其他和經濟效率相悖的政策目標，且這類產業（教育、建設、醫療、公用事業）佔國家的 GDP 比例非常高。

    • In industries run or heavily managed by the government, technological change happens slowly.
    • Example: Soviet Union.
    • Politicians often have public policy goals(, such as maximising employment,) that are inconsistent with eﬃciency.
    • Heavily regulated industries make up a big chunk of Western economies today……

    Paragraph 13 ~ 14

    討論人工智慧和疫情是否可以造成影響力：

    • 人工智慧
      • (+) 使用範圍廣泛，不需要更多的管理、可以結合現成的工具 (Word, GitHub Copilot)
      • (-) 收集資料耗費成本，建置模型昂貴
    • 疫情
      • 提高了西方國家的商業動能
      • Covid-19 may have also injected a dose of dynamism into Western economies……

    總結：要等到企業完全因應新科技而重整時 AI 的優勢才能完全顯現。這類科技的成本未來會下降，需要數年才有辦法達到大量被使用的等級。然而，許多企業仍然擔心他們資料的安全與隱私，高達 40% 的美國小型企業對採用人工智慧沒有興趣。

    • Could AI break the mould, diﬀusing across the economy faster than other re cent technologies? Perhaps. For almost any ﬁrm it is easy to dream up a use case.

    • AI can also be incorporated into existing tools.

    • On the other hand, the most signiﬁcant beneﬁts from new forms of AI will come when ﬁrms entirely reorganise themselves around the new technology;

    • The technology is undoubtedly revolutionary. But are businesses ready for a revolution?
